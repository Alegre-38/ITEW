About Tokyo

Tokyo is a vibrant metropolis that seamlessly blends ancient traditions with cutting-edge technology, offering visitors a unique cultural experience.

From historic temples and gardens to futuristic skyscrapers and neon-lit streets, Tokyo is a city of contrasts. Experience world-class cuisine, shopping, and entertainment while immersing yourself in Japanese culture.

Weather

Tokyo has four distinct seasons. Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) are the most pleasant times to visit.

Top Attractions

Shibuya Crossing

The world's busiest pedestrian crossing, surrounded by neon signs and giant video screens. A perfect spot to experience Tokyo's energy.

Senso-ji Temple

Tokyo's oldest temple, located in Asakusa. Experience traditional Japanese culture and shop for souvenirs in Nakamise-dori.

Shinjuku Gyoen

A beautiful park featuring traditional Japanese, English, and French gardens. Perfect for cherry blossom viewing in spring.

Local Tips

Food & Drink

Try authentic sushi, ramen, and tempura. Visit local izakayas for drinks and small plates. Don't miss the famous Tsukiji fish market.

Shopping

Explore Akihabara for electronics, Harajuku for fashion, and Ginza for luxury brands. Visit department stores for unique souvenirs.

Cultural Etiquette

Remove shoes when entering homes and some restaurants. Learn basic Japanese phrases. Carry cash as some places don't accept cards.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March - May)

Cherry blossom season, mild temperatures, and perfect for outdoor activities.

Autumn (September - November)

Pleasant weather, beautiful fall colors, and fewer crowds than spring.

Winter (December - February)

Cool temperatures, holiday illuminations, and great for indoor activities.
