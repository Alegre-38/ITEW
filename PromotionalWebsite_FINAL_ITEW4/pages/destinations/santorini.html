About Santorini

Santorini is a stunning Greek island in the Aegean Sea, famous for its white-washed buildings, blue-domed churches, and breathtaking sunsets over the caldera.

The island's unique landscape was formed by a volcanic eruption, creating dramatic cliffs and stunning beaches. Santorini is renowned for its luxury accommodations, world-class wineries, and romantic atmosphere.

Weather

Santorini enjoys a Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers and mild winters. The best time to visit is from May to October.

Top Attractions

Oia Village

Famous for its stunning sunsets, white-washed buildings, and blue-domed churches. The perfect place to experience authentic Greek island life.

Red Beach

One of Santorini's most famous beaches, known for its unique red volcanic sand and dramatic cliffs.

Ancient Akrotiri

A well-preserved prehistoric settlement that was buried under volcanic ash, often called the "Greek Pompeii".

Local Tips

Food & Drink

Try local specialties like fava, tomatokeftedes, and fresh seafood. Don't miss the famous Santorini wines, especially Assyrtiko white wine.

Shopping

Look for handmade jewelry, local ceramics, and traditional Greek products. The main shopping areas are in Fira and Oia.

Cultural Etiquette

Dress modestly when visiting churches. Tipping is appreciated but not mandatory. Learn a few basic Greek phrases.

Best Time to Visit

Peak Season (June - August)

Warmest weather, perfect for beach activities. Book accommodations well in advance.

Shoulder Season (April - May, September - October)

Pleasant temperatures, fewer crowds, and better prices. Ideal for sightseeing.

Off Season (November - March)

Quieter atmosphere, lower prices, but some businesses may be closed.
